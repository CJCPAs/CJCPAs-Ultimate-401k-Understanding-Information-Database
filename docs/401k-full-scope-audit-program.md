## Program Overview

### Full Scope vs. Limited Scope Audits

| Aspect | Limited Scope | Full Scope |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Investment testing | Excluded (rely on certification) | Full substantive testing required |
| Opinion type | Disclaimer on F/S as a whole | Unqualified/qualified opinion |
| When required | Plan elects ERISA 103(a)(3)(C) | No valid certification available |
| Fee level | Lower | Higher |
| Audit risk | Lower (investments excluded) | Higher (investments included) |

### When Full Scope is Required

A full scope audit is required when:
- No certification is obtained
- The certification does not meet DOL requirements
- The certifying institution is not a qualifying institution
- The certification does not cover all investments
- Investments are held outside the certified trust
- Plan administrator elects full scope
